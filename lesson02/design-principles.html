Visual Hierarchy

New York Times

Visit Website

The New York Times website is a great example of visual hierarchy. When you visit the site, your eyes are immediately drawn to the large headline at the top of the page. This headline typically highlights the most important news of the day, setting the tone for the reader. Smaller headlines, images, and teasers for other articles are presented in descending order of importance, guiding the reader through the content.

White Space and Clean Design

Google

Google

Google's homepage is the epitome of clean design and effective use of white space. The majority of the page is white space, with a simple search bar and a couple of buttons as the primary focus. This minimalistic design allows users to focus on the task at hand without distractions. The use of white space makes the interface feel approachable and user-friendly.

PARC: Proximity, Alignment, Repetition, and Contrast

Apple

Apple website

Apple's website exemplifies the principles of PARC. Proximity is evident in the way related elements, such as product images and descriptions, are grouped together. Alignment is carefully maintained throughout the site, creating a visually pleasing and organized layout. Repetition is seen in the consistent use of Apple's branding elements like fonts, colors, and product imagery. Contrast is used effectively to make key information, such as product prices and call-to-action buttons, stand out from the rest of the content.
